Knicks beat Celtics, advance to Eastern Conference finals

The New York Knicks punched their ticket to the Eastern Conference Finals for the first time since 2000 with a 119-81 win over the Boston Celtics in Game 6. There was no need for clutch heroics but Jalen Brunson met the moment. He got his teammates involved early and picked his spots, scoring 23 points on an efficient 8-14 from the field and dropping six dimes. Karl-Anthony Towns was aggressive from the outset, scoring 21 points on 8-20 shooting and grabbing 11 boards. OG Anunoby and Mikal Bridges scored 23 and 22 points respectively, not to mention Josh Hart recording the Knicks first postseason triple-double since 1972, sending a raucous Madison Square Garden home happy.

Player of the Day: Jalen Brunson
